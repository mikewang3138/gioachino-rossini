Champion Reveal: Jhin, the Virtuoso

The Composer

Gioachino Rossini

Gioachino Rossini was born on February 29, 1792, Pesaro, Papal States and died November 13, 1868 at the age of 76. He is an Italian composer known for his operas, call attention to The Barber of Seville, Cinderella , and Semiramide. Of his most popular operas, the most broadly noted is William Tell.

Education

Kyle went in to his college career set on getting his PhD in Physics... Until he discovered that physics research was incredibly boring. Now, he sets out to take the skills that he learned from a degree in physics elsewhere, ready to add a new point of view.


One of the most important things that all physics students learn is the ability to solve problems... that's kinda what the whole field is about, actually. What better place to implement this ability than The Rift. From the ever changing meta, to careful champion balancing acts, to "WHY HASN'T EKKO BEEN NERFED YET", there will always be problems that need solving in League of Legends.

Designing

The process of design and ideation has always been really interesting to Kyle. It doesn't really matter what he is designing, from coffee tables, to programs for his Computer Science classes, to 3D models to be sent to a printer, Kyle loves design. Like we said before, Kyle likes a good problem to solve. Making a design from start to finish is really just solving one problem after another. And thats like crack to Kyle.


Kyle knows that making a simple Java game, or building a table out of wood, or 3D printing some cool desk toys don't give exactly the skills necessary to make changes to Summoners Rift. However, what they show (or what Kyle hopes they show) is a willingness and passion for making things. And at the end of the day, isn't that what being a game designer for Riot is: making a great game?

Video Gaming

Kyle hasn't applied to many video game companies, but it feels right to talk about video games here. Growing up, video games were a huge part of Kyle's life. Some of his best childhood memories come from video games, like the time Kyle and all his friends set up a tarp over the back wall of his house with a projector aimed at it and played hours of 15ft Halo 2. Or running home from school to spend countless hours in Halo 3 forge mode.


As he grew up, Kyle began to appreciate video games in a whole new light. He started thinking about level design, and started noticing all of the clever tricks that game developers use. One thing that stands out are the commentary options on games like Portal. Kyle loved hearing from the devs themselves how they created the game he was playing, it felt like looking behind the curtain.


That's one of the things Kyle loves about League and Riot. The design team interacts with the community directly on the forums and Reddit. It would be an incredible opportunity to be on the other side of that conversation.
